## The Real Problem Founders Face

Automation tools are everywhere.
Ownership is not.

Most growing companies suffer from the same hidden issues:

* Automations that “exist” but aren’t trusted
* Leads not followed up on because something failed silently
* Tools that don’t talk to each other
* Critical workflows only one person understands
* Founders stepping in daily to “fix things”

When automation requires supervision, it hasn’t actually replaced the work — it has just changed it.

This creates **low-grade operational anxiety** that never goes away.

## What Happens When Something Breaks

This is the most important part.

**TheOneEye owns the failure.**

* Every system has a designated owner within our team
* When a workflow fails or behaves unexpectedly, the responsible person is notified
* The team investigates and fixes the issue
* Clients are not expected to debug workflows, retry jobs, or monitor executions

Automated notification and escalation systems are actively being developed to further reduce response time — but accountability already exists at the human level.

You are not the emergency contact.
We are.
